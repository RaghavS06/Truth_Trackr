Frequently Asked Questions

1: How does your system determine if a news article is true or false?
Our system utilizes a sophisticated machine learning model that has been trained on a vast dataset of reliable and unreliable news sources. It examines various factors, including the credibility of the source, factual content, and linguistic patterns to make its determination.
2: What sources and data does your model rely on for its evaluations?
Our model draws on a dataset of news sources, both reputable and unreliable. It uses a wide range of features, including known fact-checking databases and historical data, to make informed decisions.
3: Can your system analyze news articles from any language or region?
Currently, our system primarily focuses on news articles in English, but we are actively working on expanding its language and regional coverage. We're committed to making our service more accessible globally.
4: How long does it take for your system to evaluate a news article?
The evaluation time can vary depending on the complexity of the article and server load. Typically, it takes just a few seconds to analyze an article, but during peak times, it might take a bit longer.
5: Can your system be used for academic or research purposes?
Yes, our service can be valuable for academic and research projects. Feel free to contact us for more information about academic or research licensing.
